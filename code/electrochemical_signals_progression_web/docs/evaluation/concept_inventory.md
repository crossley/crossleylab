# Passive Signalling Concept Inventory

Use as a pre-test, post-test, or delayed retention quiz. Recommended time: 8-12 minutes.

## Student Copy

Name/ID: ____________________  Date: ____________________  Course: ____________________

For each item, circle the best answer.

### 1. Free diffusion

A group of uncharged particles starts concentrated in one part of a chamber. No membrane or field is present. What is the best description of what happens over time?

A. The particles move until they stop at equilibrium.
B. The particles spread out because they keep moving randomly.
C. The particles move only if there is a concentration gradient at every moment.
D. The particles move outward because they repel one another.

### 2. Diffusion at equilibrium

At diffusion equilibrium across a space with no electrical field, which statement is most accurate?

A. Particle motion stops.
B. Net flux is zero, but individual particles still move.
C. There is still a net flux from high to low concentration.
D. Concentration becomes zero everywhere.

### 3. Membrane pore width

Two membranes are identical except that one has a narrow pore and the other has a wide pore. All else is equal. Which outcome is most likely?

A. The wider pore produces faster redistribution across the membrane.
B. The wider pore changes the final equilibrium concentration but not the rate.
C. The narrow pore produces faster redistribution because it constrains motion.
D. Pore width should not matter if diffusion is random.

### 4. Selective permeability

A membrane is permeable to ion X but not ion Y. Initially, both are more concentrated on the left than the right. What happens first?

A. Both ions diffuse rightward at the same rate.
B. Only ion X shows substantial net redistribution across the membrane.
C. Neither ion moves because charge would be separated.
D. Ion Y moves first because it is trapped.

### 5. Adding an electrical force

A positively charged ion diffuses in the presence of a negative electrical source. Which statement is best?

A. Diffusion stops and only electrical movement remains.
B. Random motion and electrically biased drift both contribute to movement.
C. The ion moves randomly unless it touches the source.
D. The electrical source changes concentration but not movement.

### 6. Opposing forces

For one ion species, the concentration gradient favors movement out of the cell, while the electrical force favors movement into the cell. What does equilibrium mean here?

A. The ion stops moving.
B. The electrical force disappears.
C. The opposing influences balance so net flux is zero.
D. Concentrations become equal on both sides.

### 7. Nernst reasoning

The Nernst equilibrium potential for an ion is best interpreted as:

A. the membrane voltage at which that ion has no net flux
B. the voltage that stops all ions from moving
C. the voltage that equalizes all ion concentrations
D. the voltage produced only by active transport

### 8. Changing permeability in a two-ion system

If a membrane becomes more permeable to K+ relative to Na+, what should happen to the resting potential, assuming the usual concentration asymmetries?

A. It should shift toward the K+ equilibrium potential.
B. It should shift toward zero in every case.
C. It should become independent of ion concentrations.
D. It should shift away from both ion equilibrium potentials.

### 9. Multi-ion resting potential

Why is the resting membrane potential usually not equal to the equilibrium potential of just one ion?

A. Because membrane voltage is unrelated to ion movement
B. Because multiple ions contribute, weighted by their permeabilities
C. Because only negative ions determine resting potential
D. Because diffusion matters only before the membrane forms

### 10. Interpreting a stable simulation trace

In a simulation, particles continue crossing through channels in both directions, but the membrane-potential trace has stabilized near a constant value. What is the best interpretation?

A. The system is still far from equilibrium because particles are moving
B. The voltage is frozen by the plotting code
C. The system may be at dynamic equilibrium with balanced opposing fluxes
D. The channel has become impermeable

## Scoring

- Score 1 point per correct answer.
- Total score range: 0-10.
- For a shorter quiz, use:
  - Items 1-4 for diffusion and permeability
  - Items 5-7 for electrochemical and Nernst concepts
  - Items 8-10 for resting potential and Goldman-style reasoning

## Misconceptions Targeted

- Items 1-2: equilibrium means no movement
- Items 3-4: permeability affects only final state, not rate or access
- Items 5-6: electrical and chemical forces cannot act together
- Item 7: Nernst applies to all ions at once
- Items 8-9: resting potential is caused by only one ion
- Item 10: continued particle motion means equilibrium has not been reached

## Optional Short-Answer Prompts

Use 1-2 of these if you want richer evidence of mental models.

1. In one sentence, explain why a membrane potential can be stable even while ions still move.
2. In one sentence, explain why changing permeability can change resting potential.
3. Describe one way the simulations simplify real nerve-cell passive signalling.
